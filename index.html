Verbs

tell what the subject does or what the subject is. lets us know what happens. Hint* ask yourself what happened?

Verb Infinitive

is, was, has beent, etc.

Auxillary Verbs aka helping verbs. For example; could

work to form various tenses for example have be will(shall) and do

Adjectives

words that modify or describe nouns. They come before the word they modify. It can answer the question Which One? What kind Of? How many?

Adverbs

words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. they often tell how, when where, or to what degree.

4 types of Sentences

  1. Simple
  2. Complex
  3. Compound
  4. Compound-Complex

Sentence

A sentence is group of words that must contain a subject and a predicate, while conveying a meaning

Subject

tells the reader who or what is acting in the sentence

Predicate

tells the reader what the subject is doing

Sentences can have more than one subject and more than one preidcate

Simple Sentences

Contains only one independent clause.

Sentence Fragments

dont contain a subject or a predicate

Clause

a group of related words containing a subject and a verb(or subjects and verbs)

Dependent Clause

a clause that depends on an independent clause

example. after bob ran

Independent Clause

a clause that can stand alone

he caught up to the bus
